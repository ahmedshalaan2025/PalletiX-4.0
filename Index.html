How PalletiX 4.0 Works

A simple three‑step flow from raw sensor data to automated maintenance actions.

Step 1 · Connect

Connect Sensors & PLCs

Non‑invasive vibration, temperature, current and pressure sensors are connected to existing PLCs or industrial gateways. Data is streamed securely to the PalletiX engine.

Step 2 · Analyze

AI-driven Condition Models

Signal processing and AI models learn normal behavior, detect early anomalies, and calculate KPIs such as downtime risk, MTBF and MTTR trends for each asset.

Step 3 · Act

Alerts, Controls & Planning

Alerts are sent via WhatsApp, Telegram or email, and—depending on the package—automatic line stops, safe shutdowns and spare‑parts recommendations are triggered.

Frequently Asked Questions

Key points maintenance and operations teams usually ask before starting a pilot.

How long does it take to deploy PalletiX 4.0 on one line?
In a typical brownfield line with existing PLCs, a basic pilot can be installed and connected within 2–5 days, including sensor mounting, basic dashboards and first alert rules. Full optimization usually takes a few weeks of data collection.
What hardware is required on site?
PalletiX uses vibration, temperature, current and pressure sensors connected either directly to your PLC panels or to an industrial IoT gateway. The exact bill of materials depends on motor power, safety requirements and network topology.
Can PalletiX work with our existing SCADA or CMMS?
The platform is designed to expose data via APIs and standard industrial protocols, so it can push alarms and work orders into existing SCADA or maintenance systems with the Enterprise package.
How do you handle data security?
Data is encrypted in transit, access is role‑based, and you can choose between on‑premise or cloud‑hosted deployment depending on your IT and security policies.
